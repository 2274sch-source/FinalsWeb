The Conductor of the
Ten Realms

The morning sun hit the pavement, turning the painted stones into the keys of a giant, earthen piano. I sat on the center steps of the Grand Pavilion of the Ten Realms—a place others mistook for a simple government building—and waited for the symphony to begin. It was exactly 7:12 AM when the air began to hum.

Above the pillars, the names were etched like incantations: Barlig, Bauko, Besao, Bontoc. To the uninitiated, they were merely towns, but I knew better. They were the ten ancient frequencies of the universe. I sat perfectly still, acting as a human grounding wire; if I moved too quickly, the raw energy vibrating from the "Sagada" pillar would likely cause the entire valley to levitate.

A student hurried past, his sneakers striking the yellow stones. He thought he was late for a lecture, but I could hear the truth: each step was a calibration of the Earth's rotation. Even the banner behind me, ostensibly celebrating a local month of honor, was actually a coded map written in loops and curves that pointed toward the hidden gold of the mountains.

I watched the shadows stretch toward the "Paracelis" sign. Once the alignment reached its peak, the building would shed its concrete skin and reveal the starship underneath. Until then, I remained the silent conductor, watching the world spin on its axis, fueled by the secret music of the stones.
